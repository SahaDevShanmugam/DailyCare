# Heart Failure: Clinical Reference and Lifestyle Guidelines

## Definitions and stages

- **Heart failure (HF)**: The heart cannot pump enough blood to meet the body’s needs. It can be due to reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF), preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF), or mid-range (HFmrEF).
- **NYHA class I**: No limitation; ordinary activity does not cause symptoms.
- **NYHA class II**: Slight limitation; comfortable at rest; ordinary activity causes symptoms.
- **NYHA class III**: Marked limitation; less than ordinary activity causes symptoms; comfortable at rest.
- **NYHA class IV**: Symptoms at rest; any activity increases discomfort.

## Symptom patterns to watch

- **Worsening shortness of breath** (especially at rest or at night) can indicate fluid overload or worsening HF.
- **Sudden weight gain** (e.g. >2 kg in a few days) often means fluid retention; report to the care team and consider stricter fluid/salt and diuretic adjustment.
- **Swelling** (legs, ankles, abdomen) is common in HF; increasing swelling should be reported.
- **Fatigue, reduced exercise tolerance**: Track daily; a clear drop may signal worsening HF or anemia.
- **Cough, especially when lying down**: Can be related to pulmonary congestion.
- **Dizziness, fainting**: May relate to low BP, arrhythmias, or over-diuresis; report to the care team.
- **Chest pain or palpitations**: Should be reported promptly.

## Lifestyle recommendations (evidence-based)

### Diet and fluid

- **Sodium**: Limit sodium intake; often 2 g/day or less for those with fluid retention (as advised by the care team).
- **Fluid**: Many patients are advised to limit fluid (e.g. 1.5–2 L/day); follow the limit given by the care team.
- **Alcohol**: Limit or avoid; can worsen HF and interact with medicines.
- **Balanced diet**: Emphasize vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean protein; support weight and blood pressure goals.

### Activity and rest

- **Regular, moderate activity** (e.g. walking) is encouraged unless the care team advises otherwise; improves symptoms and quality of life.
- **Rest when needed**; avoid overexertion when short of breath or very fatigued.
- **Sleep**: Elevating the head (e.g. extra pillow) can help if breathless at night.

### Weight and vitals

- **Daily weight**: Weigh at the same time each day (e.g. after waking, after voiding, before eating). Report a gain of >2 kg in a few days or as advised.
- **Blood pressure and heart rate**: Take as directed; keep a log and share with the care team.

### Smoking and vaccinations

- **Smoking**: Stop smoking; it worsens heart and blood vessel health.
- **Vaccinations**: Keep flu and COVID-19 vaccinations up to date; pneumonia vaccine as recommended.

### Medication adherence

- Take all prescribed HF and other medications as directed; do not stop or change dose without the care team’s approval.
- Report side effects (e.g. dizziness, low BP, worsening kidney tests, electrolyte issues) so the regimen can be adjusted safely.

## When to seek urgent care

- Severe or sudden shortness of breath.
- Chest pain or pressure.
- Fainting or near-fainting.
- Rapid weight gain (e.g. >2 kg in a short time) with increased swelling or breathlessness.
- Confusion or sudden weakness.
- Severe dizziness or very low blood pressure with symptoms.
- Signs of infection (e.g. fever, worsening cough) in someone with HF.

These guidelines are for general education only and do not replace a healthcare provider’s advice.
